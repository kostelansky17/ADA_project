Forests as a product

Wood products are integral part of our daily lives. We have been using this material from beginning of our kind, but the demand has never been this big. The production of wood products, on average, has increased by two hundreds million m3 in the past 20 years. And this trend doesn't seem to stop. The forests Co2 on our planet and with every sawn tree this number decreases. The production of wood-based panel has increased by alarming 160 percent from 1995. The growth is steady, only significant influence which was able the affect the wood production in the world was the economic crisis in 2007-2009.

Cut down all over the world

The leading continent in production primary forest products have been in Asia followed by Northern America nad Europe, but the impact of wood production on deforestation is clearest in South America and Africa. In these two continents has the production increased by almost 30 percent and the area of forested areas decreased by 7, respectively 6 percent. Let's take a closer look at countries which most to the production of roundwood.

7 countries = 50% of world production

World production of roundwood is occupied by limited countries and the top seven countries is resposible for more than a half of the production. In the list of top producers, we can find both top deforestated (e.g. Brazil, Indonesia) and reforestated (e.g. US, China) countries. Thus, the amount of forest products is correlated to shirinkage of forest areas in some regieon. It is, however, not the case everywhere in the world. Like US and India, there surely are countries that balance both wood production and reforestation out.
